The Man Who Said
You Can Receive Help

The message of Bruno Gröning

Imagine...

You're sick. You've been sick for a long time.
You've tried everything. Nothing has worked.

And then someone says:
"There's a man. He doesn't prescribe anything.
He just says — you can be helped."

Herford, Germany — 1949

Thousands came to hear one man speak.

Not because they were gullible.
Because they were desperate.

His name was Bruno Gröning.

The Modern Problem

We treat the human being as a machine.

Something breaks — you fix the part.

But most of us sense that something is missing from this picture.

What if help is available
from a source we've learned to ignore?

Bruno Gröning

  • Born 1906 in Danzig
  • Not a doctor. Not a priest.
  • No institution behind him.
"Help is available. You can receive it.
But you have to turn away from what is weighing you down,
and open yourself to the good."

The Heilstrom

"Healing stream" — a current of goodness,
a spiritual energy that is always present.

His Teaching

  • Let go — stop clinging to what's wrong
  • Turn toward the good — as an inner act of trust
  • Receive — through openness, not effort

The Evidence

People reported healings with no medical explanation.

Today, the Circle of Friends of Bruno Gröning documents these reports with the rigor of clinical case studies.

An Ancient Pattern

"The kingdom of God is within you."
"Your faith has made you well."

The Pattern

  • There is a source of good greater than us
  • We can connect to it
  • It requires an inner turning — a letting go — a trust

This idea keeps appearing — across centuries, cultures, and people.

Why I'm Here

I'm a software engineer. 25 years building technology.
Logic, systems, code.

But I've also been drawn to a different question:
What is the human being, really?

When I encountered this teaching,
something rang true.

I've experienced help. Quiet, unmistakable.

I'm not asking you to take my word for it.
I'm telling you what I found.

Help is available —
if we learn how to receive it.

Not that you have to earn it.
Not that you have to be worthy.

Just that there is a goodness flowing through this world,
and when a person opens themselves to it —
something can begin to change.
