# Research & References

## Bruno Gröning — Key Facts
- **Born:** 1906, Danzig (now Gdańsk, Poland)
- **Died:** 1959
- **Known for:** Spiritual healing phenomenon in postwar Germany
- **1949 Herford:** Thousands gathered due to reports of healings
- **Core teaching:** The Heilstrom (healing stream) — a divine healing force available to all
- **Circle of Friends:** Continues his work today through lectures, community groups, reported healings
- **Medical-Scientific Group (MWF):** Systematically records and reviews healing reports with medical documentation

## Core Teaching Principles
1. Turn away from the bad (negativity, illness-fixation)
2. Open yourself to the good
3. Receive the healing current (Heilstrom)
4. Trust in divine order
5. Right inner attitude — not willpower, but receptivity

## Parallels with Jesus's Teachings
| Theme | Jesus | Bruno Gröning |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| Divine help | God's grace | Heilstrom |
| Healing | Faith brings healing | People receive healing |
| Inner openness | "Let those who have ears hear" | Open yourself to receive |
| Not fixating on illness | "Take up your bed and walk" | Do not cling to the illness |
| Living force | Living water, Holy Spirit | Healing stream |

**Key framing:** Not "Gröning = Jesus" but rather: spiritual truth appears again and again through different people in different times. What Gröning did was express these ideas in language that felt immediate and alive for modern people.

## Safe way to express the comparison
> "When I first encountered Bruno Gröning's teaching, what struck me was how familiar it felt.
> Many of the ideas — openness to divine help, the possibility of healing, the importance of inner alignment — reminded me of things that appear in the teachings of Jesus.
> What Gröning seemed to do was express these ideas again in language that felt immediate and alive for people in the modern world."
